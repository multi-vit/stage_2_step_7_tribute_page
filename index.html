Dr. Maurice Hilleman

Father of the Modern Vaccine

Here's a timeline of Maurice Hilleman's life:

  • 1919 - Born near Miles City, Montana
  • 1927 - Nearly dies from Diphtheria
  • 1941 - Gains bachelor’s degree majoring in chemistry and microbiology, graduating top of his class, from Montana State University
  • 1944 - Receives doctorate in Microbiology from University of Chicago and helps develop vaccine for Japanese Encephalitis, to protect American troops during World War 2
  • 1948 - Starts work at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, becoming an authority on mutation in influenza viruses
  • 1953 - Identifies a new type of virus: Adenovirus
  • 1957 - First person to predict a pandemic by identifying a new type of influenza virus, Asian influenza. Develops a vaccine estimated to have saved 1 million US lives
  • 1960 - Discovers Simian Viruses contaminating vaccines, particularly SV40 which could cause cancer
  • 1967 - Develops a Mumps vaccine after his daughter caught it in 1963, a record for fastest development of a vaccine surpassed only by the Covid-19 vaccine
  • 1968 - Develops Hong Kong Flu vaccine
  • 1969 - Develops Rubella vaccine
  • 1971 - Creates Measles, Mumps and Rubella vaccine (MMR)
  • 1981 - Develops vaccine for Hepatitis B
  • 1985 - Retires from Merck at age 65 as required by company policy, but the company rehired him to continue as a ‘consultant’ rather than employee. Works for a further 20 years (until his death) as director of the Merck Institute for Vaccinology
  • 1988 - Receives America’s highest award for science – the National Medal of Science
  • 1995 - Hepatitis A vaccine licensed after decades of work
  • 2005 - Dies of cancer at age 85

“If I had to name a person who has done more for the benefit of human health, with less recognition than anyone else, it would be Maurice Hilleman. Maurice should be recognized as the most successful vaccinologist in history.” Robert Gallo, Co-Discoverer of the AIDS Virus 1999
